kAI Lab

The kAI Lab, led by Dr. Kaiqun Fu, focuses on advancing artificial intelligence, data mining, and machine learning techniques for analyzing complex spatiotemporal systems. Our lab develops innovative computational models and algorithms to tackle real-world challenges in transportation, public safety, environmental monitoring, and socio-economic systems.

Research Interest

Spatiotemporal Data Mining

Developing models for event detection, prediction, and analysis using large-scale geospatial and time-series data.

Graph Neural Networks (GNNs)

Exploring GNN architectures for applications in intelligent transportation, social networks, and urban analytics.

Machine Learning for Intelligent Transportation Systems

Enhancing traffic incident prediction, road network modeling, and urban mobility analysis.

Multimodal Data Fusion

Integrating textual, visual, and structured data for improved forecasting and decision-making.

Physics-Informed Machine Learning

Applying deep learning techniques to solve scientific problems in power grids, environmental dynamics, and engineering systems.

AI for Social and Economic Impact

Investigating the role of AI in understanding collaboration patterns, economic trends, and public health disparities.

Lab Members & Collaboration

The kAI Lab actively trains Ph.D., M.S., and undergraduate students, fostering interdisciplinary research with experts in computer science, engineering, transportation, and public health. Our alumni and collaborators contribute to academia, industry, and government agencies.
