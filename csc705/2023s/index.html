CSC705: Design and Analysis of Computer Algorithms

Course Description: Design and analysis of algorithms to determine their time and space requirements. The study of efficient algorithms for various computational problems. Analysis of specific algorithms for internal sorting, hashing, and string search. Sorting manipulation of data structures, graphs, matrix multiplication, the Fast Fourier Transform, arithmetical operations and pattern matching. Study and implication of advanced topics on lists, stacks, trees, sets and dynamic allocation.

Prerequisites: CSC300 or any undergraduate data structures course.

Time

Tuesday & Thursday 14:00 to 13:15

Textbook

Thomas H. Cormen, Charles E. Leiserson, Ronald L Rivest, and Clifford Stein; Introduction to Algorithms, 3rd Edition. The MIT Press, 2009

Course Objectives

A student who successfully completes this course should be able to analyze algorithms and design new algorithms:

  • Ch. 1 – 3 (Role of Algorithms in computing, insertion sort, analyzing/designing algorithms, growth of functions)
  • Ch. 4, 6, 7(Divide-and-Conquer, Heapsort, Quicksort)
  • Ch. 8, Ch 13 (Sorting in Linear Time, Red-Black Trees)
  • Ch. 15 – 17 (Dynamic programming, Greedy algorithms, Amortized analysis)
  • Ch. 22 – 25 (Graph algorithms), Ch. 34 (NP-Completeness)
