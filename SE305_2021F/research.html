My research interests in the Discovery Analytics Center at Virginia Tech was on Spatial Data Mining and Urban Computing where I proposed state-of-the-art methods tackling open problems in Intelligent transportation systems, geographical information systems, and criminology of places.

Urban computing is experiencing an increasing amount of attention. Modern urban computing is an interdisciplinary study in which critical urban issues are studied using state-of-the-art computing technologies such as spatial data mining and machine learning. My research interests span the areas of urban computing: spatiotemporal data mining, GeoAI, social media analysis, and machine learning's application in spatiotemporal event forecasting. My studies can bring value to a wide range of crucial domains, such as social event detection and monitoring, transportation-related incident detection and impact prediction, urban safety perception, and various spatiotemporal-related interdisciplinary applications.

Dissertation

Spatiotemporal Event Prediction and Analysis with Urban Data

Information extraction and knowledge discovery from the urban environment is attracting increasing research interest. Ubiquitous sensors and abundant statistical reports provide enormous amount of heterogeneous urban data, such as road traffic data, crime report statistics, social media posts, and imagery of urban streets. With these types of heterogeneous urban data, wide opportunities of valuable and meaningful studies and inferences can be deducted.

The vast deployment of transportation traffic speed sensors and Traffic Incident Management Systems (TIMS) make the traffic speed data and traffic incident records ubiquitously accessible for the transportation operators. With the abundance of the traffic data sources, an efficient multi-task learning model can be implemented to provide an accurate prediction on incident duration.
Fu, Kaiqun. (2021). Spatiotemporal Event Prediction and Analysis with Urban Data (Doctoral dissertation).
